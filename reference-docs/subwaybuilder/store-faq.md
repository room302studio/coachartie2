# Store / Website FAQ (from subwaybuilder.com)

Real, verified answers to the questions people ask about buying and running the game.
(This is the STORE/product FAQ — for in-game mechanics like trains/signals/passengers, see faq.md.)

## Price
- **$30 on subwaybuilder.com**, **$40 on Steam.** It's $10 more on Steam because Valve takes a 30% cut — the website price is the cheaper one.
- Steam's terms forbid selling Steam keys elsewhere below the Steam price, so **buying on the website does NOT get you a Steam key.**

## Steam launch
- **Launches on Steam July 17, 2026**, for Windows, macOS, and Linux.
- The website version came out first; the Steam launch is the big public moment.

## Platforms / system requirements
- Supported: **Windows, macOS (Intel + Apple Silicon, macOS 12.0+), and Linux.**
- **NOT supported: Chromebooks and mobile devices.**
- It's lightweight: "if your computer can run Google Earth in Chrome smoothly, it can run Subway Builder."
- **Requires an internet connection** to load the map tiles.

## Updates
- Auto-updates like any normal app (checks + downloads on its own).
- Website buyers and Steam buyers get the exact same updates.

## Multiple devices / license
- Yes — you can move your license to a new device by resetting it on the **reset-license** page (subwaybuilder.com/reset-license).

## Cities
- Ships with a set of cities (see subwaybuilder.com/cities); **more cities are added over the coming months.**
- Community mods add extra cities too (see modding.md).

## Mods
- Yes, there are **community-created mods** that add features and cities.
- Find and download them in the **#mod-sharing channel on the Discord** (see modding.md for how to make/install them).

## Languages
- Available in **23 languages**: English, Arabic, Chinese, Czech, Dutch, French, German, Hindi, Hungarian, Indonesian, Italian, Japanese, Korean, Polish, Portuguese, Romanian, Russian, Spanish, Swedish, Thai, Turkish, Ukrainian, Vietnamese.

## How the simulation works (short version)
- Simulates **millions of commuters** built from **US/UK census data**, placed in homes and assigned jobs via a **distance-based gravity model**, each with realistic departure-time distributions.
- Passengers choose whether to ride based on **commute time, cost, and their value of time** (income varies across groups; college students and airport travelers have their own patterns from federal/FAA data).
- Pathfinding uses the **RAPTOR algorithm**. Delays/cancellations affect commutes, and future commuters adapt. Full detail at subwaybuilder.com/simulation.
